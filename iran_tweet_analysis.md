# Iran Twitter Data Analysis: Themes, Narratives & Messaging Patterns

**Dataset Overview**
- Total Tweets: 1,499
- Date: January 15, 2026 (single day snapshot)
- Primary Languages: English (728), Farsi (559), Spanish (115), Other (97)
- Retweet Percentage: 54% (indicating high coordination/amplification)

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This dataset captures a highly coordinated information campaign focused on protests and unrest in Iran. The messaging shows three dominant narratives competing for attention, with significant evidence of organized amplification through retweets. The campaign appears timed around a specific event ("Digital Blackout" and "Iran Revolution 2026") and shows characteristics of both grassroots activism and coordinated messaging.

---

## MAIN THEMES

### 1. **Digital Blackout & Communication Suppression** (Primary Theme)
- **Hashtag**: #DigitalBlackoutIran (128 mentions - top hashtag)
- **Narrative**: Claims of internet shutdown by Iranian authorities to suppress protests
- **Keywords**: blackout (70), internet shutdown (6), censorship
- **Framing**: Regime is hiding atrocities by cutting communications

### 2. **Revolution & Protest Movement** (Primary Theme)
- **Hashtags**: #IranRevolution2026 (100), #IranMassacre (74), #IranProtests (13)
- **Keywords**: protest (95), revolution (48), massacre (49), killed (50)
- **Narrative**: Current protests represent a revolutionary moment requiring international attention
- **Emotional Appeal**: Violence against peaceful protesters, martyrdom framing

### 3. **Monarchist Restoration** (Significant Sub-theme)
- **Hashtags**: #KingRezaPahlavi (47), #JavidShah (31), جاویدشاه# (33)
- **Keywords**: Pahlavi (89), Reza Pahlavi (45), Crown Prince (22), Shah (35)
- **Narrative**: Support for Reza Pahlavi (son of last Shah) as alternative leadership
- **Note**: "Javid Shah" means "Long Live the King" in Farsi

### 4. **Regime Criticism** (Dominant Theme)
- **Keywords**: regime (124), Islamic Republic (88), dictator (21)
- **Hashtags**: #DownWithIslamicRepublic, مرگ_بر_خامنه# ("Death to Khamenei")
- **Framing**: Current government as illegitimate, violent, terrorist-supporting
- **Target**: Khamenei (Supreme Leader) specifically mentioned ~95 times

### 5. **International Appeals** (Supporting Theme)
- **Focus**: Appeals to Trump administration, UN, Western governments
- **Keywords**: Trump (173 mentions), human rights (10), UN/Security Council (~28)
- **Narrative**: Request for international intervention or support
- **Tone**: Urgency, humanitarian crisis framing

---

## NARRATIVE FRAMEWORKS

### **Dominant Narrative Arc**:
1. Peaceful protests have erupted across Iran
2. Regime is responding with massacre and violence
3. Regime has cut internet to hide atrocities from world
4. International community must intervene
5. Reza Pahlavi represents alternative leadership

### **Counter-Narratives Detected** (Minority):
The hashtag نه_شاه_نه_شیخ# ("Neither Shah nor Sheikh") appears 6 times, representing opposition to BOTH the Islamic Republic AND monarchist restoration - advocating for democratic/republican alternative.

---

## PROPAGANDA INDICATORS & MESSAGING PATTERNS

### **Coordination Indicators**:

1. **High Retweet Percentage** (54%)
   - Suggests organized amplification rather than organic conversation
   - Top amplified accounts are political activists and news aggregators

2. **Hashtag Synchronization**
   - Multiple hashtags used together in coordinated clusters
   - Standard formula: #IranRevolution2026 + #DigitalBlackoutIran + #KingRezaPahlavi
   - Indicates possible shared messaging guides or templates

3. **Typo Variations Maintaining Same Hashtag**
   - #IranRevolution2026, #IranRevoIution2026 (with uppercase i), #IranRevelution2026
   - #DigitalBlackoutIran, #DigitalBlackoutlran (with lowercase L)
   - Suggests rushed copying/pasting or attempts to avoid hashtag filters

4. **Top Amplified Accounts**:
   - @gghamari: 28 RTs (Golbarg Golkar Hamari - Iranian-American activist)
   - @MarioNawfal: 13 RTs (Large news aggregator account)
   - @isaacrrr7: 12 RTs
   - Several Israeli/pro-Israel accounts heavily retweeted (@EYakoby, @Osint613, @HenMazzig)

### **Propaganda Techniques Identified**:

1. **Atrocity Propaganda**
   - Heavy use of "massacre" terminology
   - Personalization: naming specific victims with photos
   - Emotional appeals through martyrdom framing

2. **Urgency Manufacturing**
   - "2026" in hashtags creates time-bound crisis framing
   - "Digital Blackout" implies limited window for action

3. **Authority Bootstrapping**
   - Quotes from government officials (New Zealand MFA cited)
   - Appeals to international bodies (UN, Security Council)
   - Celebrity/influencer amplification

4. **Legitimacy Building for Pahlavi**
   - Strategic positioning as "Crown Prince"
   - "King" language despite no current monarchy
   - Trump connection emphasized (meeting references)

5. **Enemy Framing**
   - Islamic Republic portrayed as monolithically evil
   - No nuance in regime criticism
   - Linking to terrorism consistently

### **Factional Dynamics**:

The data reveals at least three competing opposition factions:

1. **Monarchists** (Dominant in this sample)
   - Pro-Pahlavi, "Javid Shah" messaging
   - ~98 pro-Pahlavi indicators vs ~14 anti-Pahlavi

2. **MEK/PMOI** (Present but minority)
   - Maryam Rajavi mentions (~25)
   - Known for organized social media campaigns
   - Historically controversial opposition group

3. **Democratic/Republican Opposition** (Minimal presence)
   - "Neither Shah nor Sheikh" hashtag
   - Rejects both current regime AND monarchist restoration
   - Very limited visibility in this dataset

## FARSI VS ENGLISH MESSAGING DIFFERENCES

### **English Tweets**:
- **Audience**: International, Western governments, media
- **Tone**: More formal, appeals to human rights frameworks
- **Content**: 81% are retweets (highly amplified)
- **Focus**: International intervention requests, Trump mentions
- **Function**: External pressure building

### **Farsi Tweets**:
- **Audience**: Iranian diaspora, Iranians with VPN access
- **Tone**: More emotional, direct confrontation
- **Content**: 91% are original tweets (more organic discussion)
- **Focus**: Internal organizing, ideological debates, direct regime criticism
- **Function**: Community mobilization, internal coordination
- **Notable**: More infighting visible (criticizing other opposition groups)

### **Key Farsi Themes**:
- اعتراضات_سراسری# (nationwide protests): 18 mentions
- قیام_تا_سرنگونی# (uprising until overthrow): 14 mentions
- مرگ_بر_سه_فاسد_ملا_چپی_مجاهد# (death to three corrupt: mullah, leftist, MEK): 6 mentions
  - Shows internal opposition divisions

## INFORMATION WARFARE ASSESSMENT

### **Likely Authentic Elements**:
- Real events appear to have occurred (protests referenced)
- Multiple independent voices with varied perspectives
- Criticism of ALL opposition groups (not just regime)
- Emotional, personal testimonies mixed with organized messaging

### **Coordinated Campaign Elements**:
- Synchronized hashtag deployment
- High retweet-to-original ratio
- Template-like messaging patterns
- Strategic timing around "2026 Revolution" framing
- Cross-platform amplification (Twitter, likely others)

### **Propaganda Characteristics**:
- **Selective Amplification**: Monarchist narrative dominates despite being one of several opposition factions
- **Simplified Enemy**: No acknowledgment of regime complexity or internal politics
- **Savior Narrative**: Pahlavi positioned as singular solution
- **Manufactured Urgency**: "2026" creates artificial timeline
- **Emotional Manipulation**: Heavy use of victim imagery and massacre language

### **Geopolitical Context**:
- Israel-linked accounts heavily amplifying (strategic interest in regime change)
- Trump administration mentions (timing with new administration)
- Appeals to Western intervention (classic regime change campaign pattern)

---

## CONCLUSION

This dataset represents a **hybrid information campaign** combining:
1. Likely genuine grievances and protest activity
2. Organized amplification by monarchist supporters
3. Strategic messaging aimed at international intervention
4. Multiple competing opposition narratives with monarchists dominating this sample

**Primary Objective**: Build international pressure for regime change by:
- Documenting atrocities
- Creating crisis narrative
- Positioning Pahlavi as alternative
- Requesting Western intervention

**Assessment**: While protests and grievances appear real, the messaging shows clear coordination and propaganda elements designed to shape international perception and policy. The campaign skillfully blends authentic voices with organized amplification to maximize impact.

The dominant narrative (monarchist restoration) represents only one faction of Iranian opposition but has achieved superior messaging coordination and international amplification in this dataset.
