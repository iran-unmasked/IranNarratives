## CONCLUSION

The Farsi tweets reveal what the English tweets hide: **this is a fractured opposition with significant internal conflicts**, not a unified movement. The monarchist faction has achieved superior message discipline and international amplification, but faces criticism from other opposition elements who view them as opportunistic or authoritarian.

The dual-language strategy is sophisticated:
- **English = International legitimacy building**
- **Farsi = Internal mobilization and debate**

Both authentic grievances and coordinated propaganda elements coexist in this dataset, making simple categorization impossible. The campaign is real, but the narrative being amplified internationally represents one faction's perspective, not the full spectrum of Iranian opposition.
